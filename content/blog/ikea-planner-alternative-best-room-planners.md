## Why People Look for an IKEA Planner Alternative

Before the alternatives, it's worth being specific about the pain points. Not all of them will apply to you, and the one that does will point you directly to the right tool.

### 1. You're not buying everything from IKEA

This is the biggest one. The IKEA planner is a sales tool first, a design tool second. Its entire furniture library is IKEA products. If you own a non-IKEA sofa, a vintage sideboard, or anything from another retailer, it simply doesn't exist in the planner. You can approximate with a generic rectangle, but that defeats the point.

### 2. The interface gets slow fast

The IKEA 3D planner is browser-based and can become painfully sluggish on complex rooms. Rotating a view, adding a fifth piece of furniture, zooming into a corner — these actions can take 3–5 seconds each. When you're trying to quickly test different arrangements, that lag kills the creative process.

### 3. Non-rectangular rooms are a fight

If your room has an alcove, a chimney breast, a bay window, or any angle that isn't 90 degrees, the IKEA planner becomes frustrating quickly. You spend more time faking the room shape than actually planning the layout.

### 4. No AI, no suggestions, no help

The IKEA planner tells you if furniture physically overlaps. It doesn't tell you if your layout makes spatial sense. There's no suggestion engine, no "this might work better," no feedback of any kind. You're on your own.

### 5. You can't export or share easily

Sharing your IKEA plan with a partner, contractor, or friend requires screenshots or a link that only works while the session is live. For a tool people use to make multi-thousand-dollar decisions, the collaboration layer is thin.

## Switching From IKEA Planner: A Practical Walkthrough

If you've already invested time in the IKEA planner and are switching tools, here's how to carry that work forward without starting from zero.

### Step 1: Note your room dimensions

Before closing the IKEA planner, open a note and write down:
- Room length and width (in cm or inches, whichever you use)
- Door and window positions and widths
- Any fixed features: radiators, outlets, chimney breast

These don't transfer automatically to any tool, so capture them manually.

### Step 2: List what stays vs what's new

Go through your IKEA plan and categorize every item:
- **Staying:** furniture you already own or definitely buying from IKEA
- **Replacing / adding:** pieces you want from other brands or already own

For items you're keeping from IKEA, note the product name — you can add them to Muebly by searching the IKEA catalog directly.

### Step 3: Re-enter in your new tool

In [Muebly](/):
1. Enter your room dimensions (the ones you noted in Step 1)
2. Add doors, windows, and fixed features
3. Search for your IKEA pieces by name — they're in the catalog
4. Add any non-IKEA pieces by brand search or custom dimensions
5. Run the AI layout suggestions to see what the tool recommends

Most people complete this process in under 30 minutes and immediately see the layout differently once everything is to scale in the same view.

### Step 4: Test arrangements before buying

The point of switching tools is to make better decisions. Use this phase to:
- Try furniture in positions you hadn't considered
- Check traffic flow (there should be at least 36 inches between major pieces)
- Confirm the TV-to-sofa distance (8–10 feet is the standard)
- See whether the rug you're considering actually anchors the seating area
