# IKEA Planner Alternatives in 2026: Better Tools for Planning Your Space

The IKEA Room Planner is many people's first encounter with digital room planning. It's free, tied to IKEA's product catalog, and gets the job done for straightforward layouts. But it has real limitations — and if you've spent more than 30 minutes fighting with it, you already know what they are.

## What the IKEA Planner Does Well

Credit where it's due: the IKEA planner is genuinely useful if:
- You plan to buy mostly or entirely from IKEA
- You're working with a fairly standard room shape
- You just need a basic sanity check on whether things fit

For that use case, it's hard to beat — especially at the price of free.

## Where It Falls Short

**It only includes IKEA products.** This sounds obvious, but it's a real constraint. Most people mix furniture from different sources. Your current sofa, a vintage find, items from other retailers — none of these exist in the IKEA planner. You end up with an incomplete picture of your actual room.

**The interface is slow and clunky.** The web-based 3D planner can be painfully slow on anything but a very fast machine. Simple operations like rotating a piece or checking clearances become frustrating.

**No AI or suggestions.** You're on your own for deciding where things go. The planner will tell you if something overlaps, but it won't tell you if your layout makes sense.

**Limited room shapes.** Rooms with angled walls, alcoves, or non-rectangular footprints are hard to represent accurately. If your space isn't a rectangle, you'll spend a lot of time workarounding the limitations.

## The Alternatives Worth Trying

### Planner 5D
One of the most established room planning tools. Has a large furniture library, supports irregular room shapes, and produces good 3D visualizations. The free tier is limited but workable for basic layouts. Best for people who want detailed 3D renders and are okay with a steeper learning curve.

### RoomSketcher
More floor-plan focused than 3D-first. Clean interface, easy to use, good for sharing layouts with contractors or partners. The free version watermarks exports, which matters if you need to print and take them to a store. Strong option if your priority is precision over visuals.

### Homestyler
AI-first approach — you can photograph your room and it will map the dimensions automatically. Good for getting started fast. The AI room generation features are impressive for inspiration, less useful for precise planning.

### SketchUp Free
Overkill for most homeowners, but if you're technically inclined, SketchUp's free web version is extremely powerful. No furniture library out of the box, but you can find models online. Steep learning curve; rewards the effort.

### Muebly
[Muebly](/) is designed specifically for homeowners who want the spatial accuracy of a professional planner without the learning curve of SketchUp or the brand lock-in of the IKEA tool. You add your room dimensions, arrange furniture from a broad catalog, and the AI helps optimize the layout. Works with furniture from any brand, not just one retailer's catalog. The interface is built to be fast — most people have a working layout within 20 minutes.

| Need | Best Option |
|------|------------|
| Only buying from IKEA | IKEA Room Planner |
| Need detailed 3D renders | Planner 5D |
| Sharing with a contractor | RoomSketcher |
| Fast AI-powered layout | Muebly |

## The Thing Most Planners Don't Tell You

The best planner is the one you'll actually finish your layout in. A more powerful tool that you abandon halfway is worse than a simpler one that gets you to a decision.

Most room planning projects fail not because the tool was bad, but because the process took too long and felt too complicated. The ideal tool is fast, gives you useful feedback, and doesn't require you to watch a tutorial before you start.

## Conclusion

The IKEA planner is a good starting point but a poor finishing line for most room planning projects. The alternatives have grown significantly, and the right choice depends on how much time you want to invest and what flexibility you need.
