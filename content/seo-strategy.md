**Version:** 2.0
**Product reality:** Upload photo → AI redesigns room → Shop real furniture
**Three distinct user flows = three distinct keyword universes**

---

## CONTEXT: WHAT MAKES MUEBLY'S SEO DIFFERENT

Most "AI interior design" tools are either pure renderers (no shopping) or pure planners (no photo input). Muebly sits at the intersection of three high-intent search behaviors:

1. **Visual transformation** — "redesign my room with AI" (creator/homeowner intent)
2. **Shopping trigger** — "shop the look", "where to buy furniture I saw" (commerce intent)
3. **Professional workflow** — real estate staging, ecommerce product visualization (B2B intent)

Each flow has its own keyword cluster, its own landing page, and its own conversion path. The strategy below maps all three.

**Critical observation:** The existing `/ecommerce` and `/real-estate` routes are money pages that are almost certainly under-optimized. These have higher purchase intent and higher LTV than homeowner traffic. They get equal weight in this strategy.

These six pages should absorb the most internal link equity and receive the most editorial attention. They are the pages that convert.

**Primary keyword:** `virtual staging software for realtors`
**Secondary:** `ai home staging free`, `virtual staging cost`, `best virtual staging app 2026`

This is the highest-LTV page on the site. Real estate agents and brokers who convert stay. The page needs to speak the RE agent's language: days on market, list price lift, cost per staged room vs. traditional staging ($150–$500/room vs. $2,000–$3,500 traditional).

- Before/after examples with MLS-ready outputs
- Testimonial from an agent, not a homeowner

**Schema:** `Service`, `FAQPage`, `AggregateRating`

**Primary keyword:** `ecommerce furniture visualization tool`
**Secondary:** `ai product photography furniture`, `3d furniture visualization`, `reduce furniture returns ai`

B2B SaaS angle. Furniture brands use Muebly to show their products in real room contexts — reducing returns, increasing conversion. This page needs a completely different tone than the consumer pages: ROI metrics, API/integration language, case study format.

**Must include:**
- "X% reduction in returns" stat (or directional claim)
- Product catalog integration angle
- Demo CTA (not "start free" — "book a demo")

## PART 3 — INTERNAL LINKING STRATEGY

### The Core Rule
Every page on the site serves one of three goals: **rank**, **convert**, or **support**. Internal links should always flow from support → rank → convert. Never link from a money page outward to a thin page.

### Link Flow Map

```
BLOG POSTS (rank)
     │
     ▼
STYLE PAGES /styles/[x] (rank + convert)
     │
     ▼
HOMEPAGE / (convert)
     │
     ├──▶ /real-estate (convert — RE segment)
     └──▶ /ecommerce (convert — B2B segment)

PROGRAMMATIC /ideas/[room-style] (rank)
     │
     ▼
STYLE HUB /styles/[x] (rank + convert)
     │
     ▼
HOMEPAGE / (convert)

COMPARISON PAGES /vs/[competitor] (rank)
     │
     ▼
HOMEPAGE / (convert)
     │
     └──▶ /pricing (convert)
```

### Anchor Text Rules

**Never repeat the same anchor text from the same page twice.** Google treats this as a signal quality issue. Rotate:

| Destination | Anchor Variants |
|-------------|----------------|
| Homepage | "Muebly", "Muebly's AI room designer", "try it free", "generate your room" |
| /real-estate | "virtual staging tool", "AI home staging", "stage homes with AI" |
| /ecommerce | "furniture visualization for brands", "show furniture in real rooms" |
| /styles/[x] | "[style name] room generator", "see your room in [style]" |
| /vs/[x] | "[competitor] vs Muebly", "how Muebly compares" |

### Pillar Pages + Supporting Cluster (Current Blog Gap)

The blog has 46 posts but **no pillar pages**. This means link equity is scattered. Fix by designating 4 pillar posts that receive internal links from all related articles:

| Pillar | Receives links from |
|--------|-------------------|
| "How AI Interior Design Works" | All AI/tech posts |
| "Virtual Staging Complete Guide" | All RE posts |
| "Interior Design Styles: Complete Guide" | All style posts |
| "Best AI Interior Design Apps 2026" | All comparison posts |

Each pillar page should link to 8–12 supporting posts, and each supporting post links back to the pillar. This creates topic clusters Google can clearly map.

### Critical: Image ALT Text as Internal Linking Signal

For a visual product like Muebly, every AI-generated room image should have an ALT text that functions as a keyword signal AND a contextual cue:

```
❌ alt="room design"
✓  alt="Scandinavian living room redesigned with Muebly AI — cream sofa, oak floors, minimal decor"
```

This is an often-ignored link between image SEO and page authority.

### Article (all blog posts — extend existing)

The blog currently generates OpenGraph metadata. Add full Article schema. The key additions are `author` (even as Organization), `dateModified` (signals freshness to Google), and `image` (enables rich snippets in news/blog carousels).

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Article",
  "headline": "{{post.title}}",
  "description": "{{post.description}}",
  "image": "{{post.coverImage}}",
  "datePublished": "{{post.date}}",
  "dateModified": "{{post.updatedAt ?? post.date}}",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Muebly",
    "url": "https://muebly.app"
  },
  "publisher": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Muebly",
    "logo": {
      "@type": "ImageObject",
      "url": "https://muebly.app/logo.png"
    }
  },

    "@type": "WebPage",
    "@id": "https://muebly.app/blog/{{post.slug}}"
  }
}
```

**Add `FAQPage` schema to any article with 3+ Q&A pairs.** This is the fastest path to SERP real estate expansion — FAQ rich results can double the vertical space your result occupies.

```json
      "@type": "Question",

      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "{{answer}}"
      }
    }
  ]
```
